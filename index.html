Sensor Lattice Consistency Analysis™

A deterministic, physics-grounded system and method for verifying compatibility between digital media and physical sensor acquisition pipelines. Designed for forensic, legal, and evidentiary use.

About Method Legal Company

Definition

Sensor Lattice Consistency Analysis (SLCA) is a deterministic analytical methodology that evaluates whether a digital artifact is consistent with the physical constraints imposed by real-world sensor acquisition systems.

SLCA does not perform classification of artificial intelligence systems, generative models, or software tools. Instead, it measures whether observable bit-level lattice behavior is compatible with physical capture.

The method operates independently of training data, probabilistic inference, or model attribution.

Methodology Summary

Physical Sensor Constraints

Physical sensors impose fixed spatial sampling, quantization, and noise characteristics that form a constrained acquisition lattice. These constraints persist through compression and post-processing.

Bit-Level Lattice Extraction

Media artifacts are reduced to deterministic bit-distribution representations that reflect underlying acquisition behavior rather than semantic content.

Reference Distance Evaluation

Extracted lattice signatures are compared against reference baselines derived from physically captured media using deterministic distance metrics.

Forensic & Legal Positioning

SLCA is structured to meet forensic reliability requirements including determinism, repeatability, transparency, and independence from adaptive learning systems.

  • Does not assert authorship, intent, or identity
  • Does not rely on artificial intelligence classification
  • Evaluates physical capture compatibility exclusively

The methodology is suitable for expert testimony, evidentiary evaluation, and technical affidavits where explainability is required.
